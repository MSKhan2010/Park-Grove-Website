Our Admission Philosophy

A thoughtful beginning, shaped around the person.

Park Grove is dedicated to facilitating a transparent and comforting transition for all new residents. Recognising that moving into a residential care home is a significant life event, we prioritise clear communication, professional assessment, and personalised support.

Every admission is handled with warmth, dignity, and patience, ensuring care needs are understood before arrival and that each person feels safe, valued, and genuinely welcomed from the very beginning.

A gentle move, a familiar face, and a place to feel at home.

Step by Step

The Admissions Process

Designed to feel clear, supportive, and reassuring from the first conversation to settling in.

01
Before Admission

Prior to Admission

To ensure a seamless transition from your present home to Park Grove, we highly encourage a preliminary visit. This is a valuable opportunity to discuss any concerns over coffee or a meal, accompanied by family or a social worker.

A comprehensive pre-admission assessment will be conducted to fully understand your personal care needs and determine the appropriate fee structure, ensuring Park Grove is the right environment for you.

02
Arrival Day

On Admission

Upon your arrival, the friendly face you met during your initial visit will be there to personally greet you. A dedicated keyworker will be assigned to work alongside you, offering as much or as little support as you request.

Your daily care programme is organised to reflect your individual needs. Clothing is permanently labelled, and flexible dining options mean meals can be enjoyed in the dining room or in the privacy of your own room.

03
Settling In

After Admission

We believe in absolute peace of mind, which is why we offer a suggested trial period of four weeks. This provides ample opportunity to experience life at Park Grove and decide if you wish to make it your permanent home.

Should you feel it is not the right fit, we will professionally assist in exploring realistic alternatives. The right to change your mind is always respected.

What families can expect

A clear process, a kind welcome, and support that continues.

Admission is not treated as a single form or appointment. It is a careful beginning, built around the resident's comfort, choices, routines, and peace of mind.
